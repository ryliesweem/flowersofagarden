What is culture?

Flowers of a Garden was created by Lily Farid, Rylie Sweem, and Natalie Musenga. This project was a culmination of our own personal reflections on culture and society. Each of us come from diverse backgrounds with diverse experiences and cultures. Together as a group we thought about how these differences in our cultures affected our roles in society. Did our differences make it harder to find a group of people or did they help us to build interesting relationships? Was culture even something that could be shared or does everyones unique experiences make the very idea of shared culture a mere illusion? And in our increasingly polarizing society, does culture band together or force us apart?

Through these deliberations we decided to do some research. We consulted people both near and distant from us to gain their perceptions of culture and society, we looked at research in the field, we found quotes from influential people from all parts of the world and all kinds of cultures. We came to the conclusion that a diversity of culture is essential for the fabric of society. Differences should not be the reason for false dichotomies between groups of people but instead an opportunity for learning, understanding and growth. When we accept the diversity of humankind we are able to work together and make tremendous leaps in any field.

This projectÂ is meant to display the diversities of humanity and show the beauty of unity in diversity. We have collected responses from those around us on the meaning and effect of culture and placed them as flowers in the garden of humanity. We chose the format of origami because origami is a very cultural thing which the members of our group relate to. It also displays the humanity of our project and the imprints that each fold of life creates on our personal cultures. As an ever growing garden, we hope that this project can celebrate the differences that make us all members of one human family.
